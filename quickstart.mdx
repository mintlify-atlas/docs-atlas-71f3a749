## Your First Question

GitaChat makes it simple to find relevant wisdom from the Bhagavad Gita. Let's walk through asking your first question.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit GitaChat">
    Go to [gitachat.org](https://gitachat.org) and you'll see the search interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Ask a Question">
    Enter any question or situation you're facing. For example:
    - "How do I deal with anxiety?"
    - "What is my purpose in life?"
    - "How can I overcome fear?"
    
    GitaChat uses semantic search to understand the meaning behind your question, not just keywords.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Results">
    Within seconds, you'll receive:
    - The most relevant verse from the Bhagavad Gita
    - **Contextual commentary** - AI-generated explanation tailored to your question
    - **Traditional commentary** - Scholarly interpretation of the verse
    - **Related verses** - Additional teachings on similar topics
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore & Save (Optional)">
    Sign up for a free account to:
    - Bookmark verses for later reference
    - Add personal notes and reflections
    - Track your search history
    - Receive daily verses via email
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Sign Up Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Sign In">
    Click the **Sign In** button in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Authentication Method">
    Sign up using:
    - Email and password
    - Google account
    - Other OAuth providers
    
    GitaChat uses [Clerk](https://clerk.com) for secure authentication.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Your Profile">
    Add your name and preferences. You're ready to start your personalized journey!
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Dual Commentary System

Every verse includes two types of commentary:

1. **Contextual Commentary** - AI-generated explanation that specifically addresses your question
2. **Traditional Commentary** - Scholarly interpretation from classical sources

This dual approach helps you understand both the universal teaching and its specific application to your situation.

**Learn more**: [Contextual Commentary](/features/contextual-commentary)
